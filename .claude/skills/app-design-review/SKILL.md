# Skill: app-design-review

## Purpose
Use this skill to review and improve AerisClaim UI so it looks premium, high-trust, and operationally excellent rather than generic AI-generated SaaS.

## When to use
- Reviewing landing page quality
- Reviewing app shell quality
- Reviewing dashboard density/readability
- Refining tables, panels, timelines, and workflows
- Evaluating hero design and motion quality

## Design principles
1. High trust over hype.
2. Dense but readable.
3. Left-aligned by default.
4. Strong visual hierarchy through typography and spacing, not decoration.
5. Motion should reinforce workflow, not distract.
6. Avoid AI slop.

## Explicit anti-patterns
- Blue/purple gradient startup look
- Glassmorphism
- Glowing blobs
- Random floating cards
- Overly rounded generic SaaS card grids
- Giant empty whitespace with weak information density
- Centered-everything marketing sections
- Chatbot-first UX patterns
- Emoji-driven interface chrome

## What good looks like for AerisClaim
- Serious enterprise operations feel
- Excellent work queue readability
- Tight but comfortable data density
- Clean tables and timeline views
- Strong evidence panel structure
- Clear deadlines and statuses
- Premium hero motion that visualizes workflow or claim movement

## Review checklist
- Does the screen feel trustworthy?
- Does it avoid generic AI startup aesthetics?
- Is the primary action obvious?
- Is the typography crisp and readable?
- Are spacing and alignment consistent?
- Are tables and panels easy to scan?
- Does motion feel restrained and useful?
- Would a skeptical VP of Logistics think this looks credible?
